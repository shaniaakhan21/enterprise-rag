ACME FINANCIAL CORPORATION
ANNUAL REPORT — FISCAL YEAR 2023

BUSINESS OVERVIEW
Acme Financial Corporation is a diversified financial services company
headquartered in New York. We provide investment management, wealth advisory,
and institutional banking services across 42 countries with $487 billion
in assets under management as of December 31, 2023.

COMPETITIVE POSITION
Our competitive advantage rests on three pillars: a proprietary quantitative
research platform AcmeAlpha, deep client relationships averaging 14 years
with our top 50 institutional clients, and a cost-efficient operating model
with an industry-leading expense ratio of 52 basis points.

EMPLOYEES
As of December 31, 2023, the company employed 8,742 full-time equivalent
employees globally, of whom 3,104 are based in the United States.

MANAGEMENT DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
Fiscal year 2023 represented strong execution against our strategic priorities.
Total revenue grew 11.8% year-over-year to $4.82 billion, driven by record
net inflows of $62 billion and disciplined fee management. Net income increased
17.7% to $930 million reflecting operating leverage and lower interest expense.
Management expects full-year 2024 revenue growth of 8-12%.

RISK FACTORS
Market Risk: A 10% decline in equity markets would reduce annual revenue by
approximately $185 million. Cybersecurity incidents could disrupt operations.
Our top 10 clients account for approximately 31% of total AUM.
Regulatory changes including SEC rules and MiFID II may increase compliance costs.

ACCOUNTING POLICIES
Revenue is recognised in accordance with ASC 606. Management fees are recognised
ratably over the performance period. The effective tax rate for fiscal year 2023
was 22.4% compared to 23.1% in 2022.

STOCK BASED COMPENSATION
Stock-based compensation expense for fiscal year 2023 was $148 million compared
to $132 million in 2022. Unrecognised compensation cost was $312 million.

DIVIDENDS
During fiscal year 2023 the company paid cash dividends of $4.20 per share
totalling $314 million in aggregate. The Board declared a quarterly dividend
increase to $1.15 per share in Q4 2023.

LONG TERM DEBT

LEGAL PROCEEDINGS
A putative class action was filed in November 2022 in the Southern District
of New York alleging violations of federal securities laws. The company believes
the claims are without merit and is vigorously defending the action.

ESG AND SUSTAINABILITY
Acme Financial is committed to net-zero carbon emissions by 2035 across
operations and by 2050 across our investment portfolio. In 2023 we reduced
Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions by 18% versus our 2019 baseline.
Our MSCI ESG Rating was upgraded to AA in 2023 from A in 2022.
40% of senior leadership roles are held by women up from 35% in 2022.
